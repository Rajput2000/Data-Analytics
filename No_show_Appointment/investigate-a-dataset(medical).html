Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics such as:

  • PatientId

  • AppointmentID

  • Gender

  • ScheduledDay

  • AppointmentDay

  • Age

  • Neighbourhood

  • Scholarship

  • Hipertension

  • Diabetes

  • Alcoholism

  • Handcap

  • SMS_received

  • No-show

    • </ul> about the patient are included in each row.

    Question(s) for Analysis¶

    1. What age group mostly show up for appointment?
    2. How those a patient ailment affects their presence for their appointment?
    3. How does a patients residence affects his/her presence for appointment?
    4. Does sending of sms improve the percentage of present patients?

Data Cleaning¶

Note: We can see from the information above, that some columns have incorrect datatypes and that their are no null value in this dataset. It is also inferred that the handcap and sms_recieved columns are interchanged because the handcap column is seen to have 5 unique values instead of 2 and sms_recieved is supposedly to have more than 2 unique values, so it is suggested that the handcap and sms_recieved columns are missed labeled in the dataset.

Conclusions¶

We can deduced that sending of messages, does not rely convince a patient to show up for appointment. Thereby the hospital can reduce cost from sending multiple messages to patient. The hospital should also pay more attention to handcap patients, in other to increase their presence for appointment. They can also find out why all patients from PARQUE INDUSTRIAL are absent so as to prevent future absence.<\p>
